## Purpose of this document

This brief locks the strategic foundation for the new Canwell Interiors website before any content, design, or build work begins.

---

## Brand architecture

Canwell Interiors and Saverys of Broadway are complementary, not competing.

- **Saverys of Broadway** is the bespoke design house. Project-led. Higher price point. Long lead times.
  *"We design and source your home for you."*

- **Canwell Interiors** is the furnishings showroom with helpful advice. Walk-in led. Accessible price point. Take it home or arrange delivery.
  *"We help you put it together yourself."*

The website must hold this distinction at all times. Canwell is a showroom, not a design house.

---

## Who Canwell is for

Five segments, all anchored within roughly 30 minutes of Broadway:

1. **Local homeowners** in Broadway, Willersey, Childswickham, Chipping Campden, Snowshill, and surrounding villages. Owner-occupiers, refurbishing or upgrading the family home.
2. **Second-home and weekend-home owners** in the Cotswolds with London or international primary residences. Time-poor, want it sorted in one trip.
3. **Holiday let and cottage owners** refurbishing rental stock on a cycle. Bulk, practical, repeat.
4. **Walk-in tourists** from Broadway High Street. Low conversion in the moment, real long-tail value if captured to email.
5. **Trade buyers**: independent designers, property developers, local builders sourcing for client projects.

Weekend walk-in trade from local and surrounding villages is the primary current revenue base across all five segments. Growing it is the priority.

---

## What the website does

**Customer job.** Prove the Canwell showroom is worth a weekend visit, before the customer defaults to driving to Cheltenham, Worcester, or buying online from John Lewis, Loaf, OKA, or similar.

**Business job.** Drive measurable, digitally attributable footfall lift, leading to measurable revenue growth.

The site is a footfall driver, not an ecommerce shop. Every page should give a local visitor a reason to come in this weekend.

## Positioning

> ### *The Cotswolds furnishings showroom. Walk in, choose, take home.*

This is the line everything hangs off. Five jobs in one sentence:

- Names the regional anchor (Cotswolds)
- Names the category (furnishings showroom, not interior design house)
- Spans the breadth (furniture, carpets, curtains, blinds, accessories, design help)
- Differentiates from Saverys (no bespoke, no waiting)
- Differentiates from John Lewis online and Loaf / OKA (you can see it, touch it, and leave with it)

## Success metrics

### North Star (12 months)

**+100% total revenue.** Doubling Canwell's revenue against the current baseline. The strategic ambition the engagement is built around.

### Proof point (90 days)

**+30% total revenue.** The trajectory signal that says the strategy is working. Roughly the compounding rate needed to hit 100% over a year, allowing for slower early months.

### Operating metrics (90 days)

Leading to lagging:

| # | Metric | Target | Time signal |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | GBP actions (directions, calls, website clicks) | +X% vs baseline | Weeks 2-6 (leading) |
| 2 | Klaviyo subscribers (engaged, local) | 500 minimum, 35% open rate or higher | Compounding |
| 3 | Weekend footfall (Jella) | +15% vs pre-launch baseline | Weeks 6-12 |
| 4 | Total revenue | +30% vs same period last year | Day 90 (lagging) |

If GBP is moving but revenue isn't, the issue is in-store conversion or basket size. If GBP isn't moving, the visibility work isn't biting yet. Each metric tells us where to act.

---

## Measurement infrastructure

To be set up as part of the new site build, before launch:

1. **Revenue baseline.** Last 90 days + same 90-day window last year (seasonality control). Signed off by Gary in writing.
2. **Average transaction value baseline.** Same windows. Tells us whether revenue is moving because of more customers or larger baskets.
3. **Attribution path.** "How did you hear about us?" captured at the till weekly + UTM-tagged ads and landing pages + GBP source data.

Without these three pieces of baseline data, the 90-day revenue claim is not defensible.
