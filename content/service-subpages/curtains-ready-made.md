intro_body: |
  Most of the time, made-to-measure is the better answer for the main rooms in a house. Ready-made earns its place when:

  You're refreshing a guest room or a holiday let where the windows are standard sizes and a six-week lead time isn't worth the wait.

  You want something now. A piece of fabric you've fallen out with, a curtain that the dog has reached, a room that needs to be ready for visitors arriving on Friday.

  You want to test a colour or pattern before committing to a made-to-measure version of the same fabric in the room next door.

brands_h2: "Measuring up before you visit"
brands_body: |
  Three measurements get you to the right pair:

  1. **The width of your pole or track**, end-to-end including any decorative finials.
  2. **The drop**: from the pole or track to where you want the curtain to end (sill, below sill, or floor).
  3. **The pole position**: ideally 6 to 8 inches above the window frame, and 6 to 8 inches wider than the frame on each side, so the curtains can pull right back.

  If you've got the old curtain to bring with you, even better.
