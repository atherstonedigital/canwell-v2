cards_h2: "Choosing the fabric"
cards:
  - title: "Velvet"
    body: "Warm, slightly formal, holds colour brilliantly. Shows marks more than other fabrics but settles into a beautiful patina. Best in sitting rooms where it isn't the family's everyday lounging spot."
  - title: "Linen"
    body: "Natural, relaxed, soft. Wrinkles easily, which some people love and others can't live with. Best for country and farmhouse interiors."
  - title: "Boucle"
    body: "Tightly looped, textured, currently very popular. More forgiving than linen, softer than tweed. Best for contemporary schemes and family rooms."
  - title: "Leather"
    body: "Wears in rather than out. Aniline leather develops a patina over years. More forgiving of dogs and children than fabric. Best for studies, dens, and rooms with a more traditional feel."
  - title: "Performance fabric"
    body: "Engineered to be stain-resistant and hard-wearing. Looks and feels like regular upholstery. Best for households with young children, dogs, or both."
  - title: "Woven and wool blends"
    body: "The middle ground. Practical, characterful, ageing well. Best for everyday family use."

why_h2: "Choosing the frame"
why_body: |
  Frames come in three broad shapes.

  **Classic.** Rolled arms, deep cushions, often a tufted back. Best in older houses and traditional schemes.

  **Contemporary.** Lower profile, sharper lines, fewer cushions. Best in newer houses and open-plan spaces.

  **Sectional and corner.** Larger format for big rooms, often configurable. Best for families and entertainment rooms.
