*Prepared by Dan Atherstone, Atherstone Digital | May 2026*
*For sign-off by Gary before Phase 3 (content) begins*

---

## How to read this document

This is the structural and SEO foundation for the new Canwell Interiors site. It defines:

1. The sitemap (what pages exist and how they relate)
2. The keyword map (what each page is built to rank for)
3. The per-page brief (intent, audience, CTA, proof, internal linking, schema)
4. The build priority (what gets built first, second, and third)

Every page on the site must be traceable to a defined audience, intent, and outcome. If a page can't be justified against the four success metrics in the strategy brief, it doesn't get built.

Keyword volumes are not given because no keyword tool has been run against the catchment. Volumes should be validated in kickoff week against Google Keyword Planner or Ahrefs, and the priority order revised accordingly. The structural and intent logic does not depend on volume.

---

## Strategic recap

Anchor everything below to:

- **Positioning:** *The Cotswolds furnishings showroom. Walk in, choose, take home.*
- **Customer job:** Prove the showroom is worth a weekend visit.
- **Business job:** Drive measurable, digitally attributable footfall and revenue lift.
- **North Star:** +100% revenue (12 months), +30% (90 days).

## URL strategy

Short, clean URLs throughout. Modern SEO does not reward keyword stuffing in URLs (e.g. /carpets-broadway-cotswolds). The keyword work lives in title, H1, body content, and schema. URLs prioritise clarity and longevity.

# Schema strategy

Schema markup is the cheap, durable SEO win that most small business sites neglect. Build it into the Astro templates so every page gets the right schema by type, with no manual maintenance.

| Schema type | Pages | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| LocalBusiness | Homepage, /visit, /contact, every location page | Local SEO foundation. Powers Google Business Profile, knowledge panel, local pack. |
| Organization | Homepage, /about | Brand entity |
| WebSite (with SearchAction) | Homepage | Sitelinks search box in Google |
| Service | Every service hub and sub | Service rich results, FAQ pulls |
| Product | Brand stockist pages where SKUs are surfaced | Product rich results |
| Brand | Brand stockist pages | Brand entity association |
| FAQPage | Service hubs, service subs, /design-help | FAQ rich results (high SERP real estate) |
| Article | Inspiration articles | Article rich results, Google Discover eligibility |
| Review / AggregateRating | Homepage, /visit (pulled from GBP) | Star ratings in SERPs |
| BreadcrumbList | All non-homepage pages | Breadcrumb rich results |

A central `schema.ts` utility in the Astro build should generate the right schema per page from a single content model.

# Internal linking strategy

The site has four internal linking patterns. Together they create the topical authority Google rewards and the discoverability customers need.

### Pattern 1: Hub-and-spoke

Every service hub links down to its sub-pages, and every sub-page links up to its hub. Same for the brands hub and brand pages, and the locations hub and location pages. No orphan pages.

### Pattern 2: Cross-service linking

Service hubs link to related service hubs where natural. Curtains links to soft furnishings (cushions, throws). Carpets links to design help. Furniture links to brand pages. This is where Canwell's "whole home, under one roof" positioning is reinforced architecturally.

### Pattern 3: Conversion routing

Every service page, brand page, and location page contains a route to /visit and to /design-help. These are the two conversion endpoints. Every page should be at most one click from each.

### Pattern 4: Content-to-commercial linking

Every inspiration article links to the two or three service hubs most relevant to its topic. The "How to dress a Cotswold cottage window" piece links to curtains, made-to-measure curtains, and blinds. This is where the soft, top-of-funnel content converts into footfall intent.
