**Phase 3 output, part 2 of 3. The brief for the showroom shoot.**
*Prepared by Dan Atherstone, Atherstone Digital | May 2026*
*For sign-off by Gary and use on the day of the shoot*

---

## What this brief does

This document defines every image the new Canwell website needs, and how those images should look and feel. The shoot has two jobs:

1. Capture the showroom in a way that makes someone in Willersey, Chipping Campden, or Cheltenham want to visit this weekend.
2. Produce enough material to fill the site for launch and the next twelve months of social, email, and ads.

It is structured to be used on the day. Print or open it on a phone, work through the shot list, tick as you go.

---

## Strategic recap

- **Positioning:** *The Cotswolds furnishings showroom. Walk in, choose, take home.*
- **Voice & tone:** Warm, plainspoken, family-run, Cotswolds-rooted, not luxury, not aspirational.
- **Customer job the imagery serves:** Prove the showroom is worth a weekend visit.

The pictures must do the same job as the words: make the showroom feel like a real, helpful, well-run place that's worth driving twenty minutes for. They should not feel like a catalogue. They should not feel like a London design house. They should feel like Canwell.

---

## Visual tone

### The feel

Warm. Lived-in. Honest. Light-flooded where possible. Real people in real settings, not models in staged perfection. Texture and material everywhere: fabric, wood, wool, leather, ceramic, stone. The kind of pictures that make you want to walk through the door and feel the throw.

### What we are not shooting

- Catalogue product-on-white (xshowhome's job, not this site's)
- High-gloss commercial luxury (Saverys' tier, not Canwell's)
- Sterile design-house minimalism
- Over-styled influencer-house perfection
- iPhone snapshots without composition

### What we are shooting

- The showroom as a space, with depth, light, and atmosphere
- Vignettes that feel like real rooms in real Cotswold homes
- Detail shots that let people feel material through the screen
- People (Gary, Gemma, the team) in their element, working, not posing
- The exterior in the context of Broadway High Street

## Reference visual style

Pull up these brands on a phone before the shoot and look at how they handle interior photography. Each captures something Canwell should aim for. None should be copied; the goal is the *register*, not the specifics.

| Brand | What to take from it |
|---|---|
| The Cotswold Company | Warm natural light, lived-in vignettes, country register |
| Loaf | Relaxed, honest, the sofa-as-it-actually-feels |
| Pooky | Lamps photographed with character, not catalogue |
| Soho Home | Confident, textured, never sterile |
| Country Living magazine | Cotswold appropriateness, not chocolate-box |
| Neptune (the home retailer) | Showroom photography that sells the space, not the SKU |

What none of these do: cold light, hard shadows, over-saturated colour, perfect symmetry, generic stock-photo polish. Match that absence.

## Technical specifications

### Camera and capture

- **Camera:** A proper camera (mirrorless or DSLR, 20MP+). iPhone 15/16 Pro acceptable for detail shots and reels, not for hero images.
- **Lens kit:** Wide angle (16-35mm equivalent) for interior wides. Mid-zoom (24-70mm equivalent) for vignettes and people. Prime if available (35mm or 50mm f/1.8 or faster) for detail and portrait.
- **Format:** RAW + JPG. RAW for editing flexibility, JPG for review on the day.
- **Tripod:** Essential for interior wides where light is mixed and shutter speeds will drop.
- **Reflector:** Useful for detail shots if budget allows. A white foam-board works.

### Light

- **Best window:** 10am to 3pm on a bright but overcast day. Direct sun creates hard shadows and uneven exposure across the showroom.
- **Showroom lights:** All on, warm. Mixed daylight + interior tungsten reads on camera as warm and inviting; turn off any cool fluorescents if present.
- **White balance:** Set to "Daylight" or 5500K. Do not auto-white-balance; consistent colour across the set is essential for fabric and carpet accuracy.

### Output

- **Aspect ratios needed:** 16:9 (hero banners), 4:5 (portrait blocks), 1:1 (social), 3:2 (general). Shoot wide and crop later; do not crop in-camera.
- **Final resolution for web:** 2400px on long edge, 80% JPG quality. Original RAW preserved separately.
- **Total deliverables target:** Roughly 250 to 350 final selected images from the shoot. Shoot 4 to 5x that volume to give selection room.

## People direction

### Gary and Gemma

- **Wardrobe:** Smart-casual. Cotswolds-appropriate, not London. Earth tones, warm neutrals, soft fabrics. No bright corporate colours, no logos, no patterns that fight the showroom palette.
- **Direction:** Natural, working, never posed. Mid-conversation, hands moving, looking at fabric, pointing at a sample, laughing at something Gary said.
- **Avoid:** Arms-folded "boss" stances. Wedding-photo head tilts. Direct-to-camera smile from behind a desk.

### Team

Same direction. If there's a team uniform or apron worn day-to-day, that's the right call (uniforms read as "real shop"). If not, smart-casual matching the showroom's register.

### Customers (if any feature)

- Written permission, full release form signed.
- Real customers, in real moments, not models.
- If staging a "consultation" with a non-customer, brief them to play a sceptical, interested customer rather than a delighted one. Believability over warmth.

## Post-production standards

Edit in a consistent style. Inconsistent editing across the site is one of the loudest amateur tells.

| Setting | Direction |
|---|---|
| White balance | Warm, around 5500K. Cooler than tungsten, warmer than midday daylight. |
| Exposure | Lift shadows gently, do not crush blacks. Hold highlights, do not blow them. |
| Colour | Accurate, especially for fabrics, carpets, and paint colours. Customers will compare to the real thing in the showroom; misleading colour kills trust. |
| Saturation | Slightly desaturated overall. Earth tones strong, no neon, no instagram-pop. |
| Sharpness | Standard. No oversharpening. |
| Retouching | Minimal. Remove obvious distractions (cables, fingerprints, dust). Do not stretch, smooth, or perfect people. |
| Style | One Lightroom preset for the whole shoot. Document the recipe so future shoots can match. |

### Export

- Hero / banner images: 2400px long edge, JPG 80%
- Body images: 1600px long edge, JPG 80%
- Thumbnails: 800px long edge, JPG 75%
- Social: 1080px square / 1350px vertical, JPG 85%
- Always export WebP versions alongside JPG for Astro to serve modern formats

## Backup plan

If anything goes wrong on the day, this is the priority order to defend.

1. **Showroom wides and category vignettes.** These are the launch-critical hero images. Get these first.
2. **People shots of Gary and Gemma.** Trust signal. Cannot launch without these.
3. **Exterior and entrance shots.** Visit-us page depends on these.
4. **Service-specific shots.** Each service hub needs at least three usable images.
5. **Detail shots.** Lowest priority on the day; can be captured in a half-day return visit if needed.

If weather wrecks the exterior shoot, do everything else and book a return half-day for exterior only.

If a team member is missing, capture their portrait separately later.

If specific brand pieces are mid-restock and not in the showroom on the day, shoot a "brand area" placeholder and return for those pieces specifically.
